They tell you that being a Lancer is about glory, about the "Pillar Protocols," and about protecting the "Greater Good." They don’t tell you what it looks like when a tactical-grade reactor vent melts the screaming face of your navigator while you're trapped in a bulkhead.

I’m the only one left.

Two days ago, the "Broken Link" were the kings of the Sector 0-G. We had a hidden base tucked inside a hollowed-out carbon shell, enough fuel to last a decade, and three wings of modified sub-compacts. We were just "independent contractors," as the Captain liked to say. The rest of the sector called us pirates.

Then the front door exploded.

THE ATTACK

It wasn't a fleet. It wasn't a Union blockade. It was six of them. Six Lancers.

They didn't broadcast a surrender demand. They just synchronized their blink-space entry and appeared inside our perimeter. I saw a Everest chassis moving so fast it looked like a glitch in the security feed—it tore through our captain like a hot knife through tinfoil.

Another everest loaded with missiles, terrifying, and silent—slipped through everyone. I watched from under the debris as it executed the crew in under six seconds. No waste. No mercy. Just math.

I had set my mech to walk forward on autopilot while its reactor went critical. From what I saw on my HUD before the signal died, the Lancers took the bait. They saw a high-energy signature charging them and focused on neutralizing the "threat." While they were busy turning my life's work into a miniature supernova, I was crawling through two feet of dust and recycled air, feeling the entire asteroid shudder under my chest.

A NEW FREQUENCY

My hands are still shaking while I type this on a stolen terminal. Everyone I knew is ash, and the base is a graveyard.

I'm done. I'm scrubbing my biometric data and heading for the Core Worlds. I’ve heard Equilibrium is hiring "Security Contractors" for some terraforming project in the Libra System. It’s corporate, it’s clean, and most importantly, it’s far away from the kind of people who pilot those machines.

If you’re out there, running raids and thinking you’re the apex predator of the Long Rim: Run. You haven't seen what happens when a real Lancer decides you're an objective.

I’m choosing life. I’m choosing the suit. I’m choosing a side that doesn't get turned into slag by a 100-ton god of metal.
