CATASTROPHE AVERTED: "VOID-STRUCK" SABOTEURS DEFEATED IN C-22 MINING DEPTHS

CERES-22 MINING OUTPOST – What was initially reported as a routine resource raid has been reclassified as a major attempted act of industrial sabotage. A violent cell of "Void-Struck" insurgents attempted to permanently collapse the primary quartz tunnels of Asteroid C-22, only to be neutralized by a local cadre of licensed Lancers in a high-stakes subterranean skirmish.

Unlike previous encounters involving light skirmishers, these insurgents utilized a fleet of modified industrial mining rigs. Heavy-duty rock-crushers and thermal cutters were jury-rigged with makeshift armor plating, turning civilian tools into lethal, albeit clumsy, siege engines.

Union investigators confirm the insurgents weren't there to steal the ore—they were there to bury it. Their objective was the "Keystone Adit," a structural weak point in the asteroid’s core.

    "They weren't looking for a payday," said Lead Foreman K. Volos. "They had thermal charges set at structural nodes. If those Lancers hadn't intercepted them, the entire sector of the mine would have buckled, venting three thousand workers into the void."

SUSPICIOUS ORDNANCE
The most alarming discovery followed the smoke-clear. While the insurgents piloted repurposed civilian machinery, the demolition charges and auto-canons found in their possession were far from "homemade."

The surviving insurgents are currently being held in stasis pods awaiting transfer to a Union Correctional Facility. When asked about the high-tech military gear found in the hands of "low-life miners," the Harrison Armory attache declined to comment, citing "ongoing internal security audits."
